Competitive Programming

Competitive Programming is a new subcommittee added in 2022. This allows members to compete in online programming competitions. Most if not all competitions allow C, C++, C#, Python, and Java. Competitions can either be a group competition or individual competition. This gives you the opportunity to show off your programming skills and train your way of thinking.

Rules

  • At least one team captian to hold practices and look for competitions to participate in.
  • Any one within ACM is allowed to participate.
  • Be respectable to others and their skill level.

Recommendations

  • Have weekly or biweekly practices/meetings that work for the most people.
  • Just because someone can't make it to meetings doesn't mean they can't be part of Competitive Programming.
  • Group people up for each practice at the start of the school year. Different groups each practice.
